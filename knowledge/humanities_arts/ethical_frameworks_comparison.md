# Ethical Frameworks — Comparative Reference

## Framework Comparison Table

| Framework | Core Principle | Decision Criterion | Key Strength | Key Weakness | Primary Thinkers |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|--------------|--------------|-----------------|
| Act Utilitarianism | Maximize aggregate happiness (hedons) | Does this act produce the greatest good for the greatest number? | Quantifiable; democratic; flexible | Justifies harming minorities; requires impossible knowledge of all consequences | Bentham, early Mill |
| Rule Utilitarianism | Follow rules that maximize utility | Would following this rule generally maximize utility? | Avoids act-utilitarianism's extreme conclusions; stable expectations | Devolves into act-utilitarianism under pressure | Mill (later), Brandt |
| Kantian Deontology | Act from duty according to universal law | Can I universalize this maxim without contradiction? | Protects individual rights absolutely; consistent | Rigid; conflicting duties unresolvable; ignores consequences entirely | Kant |
| W.D. Ross's Prima Facie Duties | Multiple duties, not one supreme principle | Which duty is most binding given ALL duties in this situation? | More flexible than Kant; intuitively plausible | No systematic resolution when duties conflict | W.D. Ross |
| Virtue Ethics | Cultivate character to be a good person | What would a virtuous (phronimos) person do? | Holistic; contextual; focuses on character development | No action-guidance algorithm; who defines virtues? culturally variable | Aristotle, MacIntyre, Foot |
| Care Ethics | Maintain caring relationships and respond to vulnerability | Does this preserve relationships and attend to the particular needs of those involved? | Contextual; relational; challenges impartiality assumption | Hard to scale beyond personal relationships; can justify partiality | Gilligan, Noddings, Held |
| Contractarianism | Rules rational self-interested agents would agree to | Would self-interested rational agents agree to this rule? | Connects morality to rational self-interest | Ignores those outside the contract (animals, future generations, disabled) | Hobbes, Gauthier |
| Contractualism | Principles no one could reasonably reject | Could this principle be justified to everyone affected? | Non-aggregative; respects each person | "Reasonable rejection" undefined; vague threshold | Rawls, Scanlon |
| Natural Law | Morality derived from human nature/reason | Does this accord with our natural end (telos) and right reason? | Objective foundation; universal applicability claimed | Naturalistic fallacy risk; requires contested metaphysics | Aquinas, Finnis |

## Kant's Categorical Imperative — Three Formulations

| Formulation | Statement | Test |
|------------|-----------|------|
| Universal Law | "Act only according to that maxim whereby you can at the same time will that it should become a universal law" | Would universalizing this maxim produce a contradiction? |
| Humanity Formula | "Act so that you treat humanity, whether in your own person or in that of another, always as an end and never as a means only" | Am I using a person purely instrumentally? |
| Kingdom of Ends | "Act according to maxims of a universally legislating member of a merely possible kingdom of ends" | Could rational agents agree to this rule in an ideal community? |

## Rawls' Theory of Justice — Key Elements

```
Original Position: hypothetical choice situation
Veil of Ignorance: don't know your place in society (class, race, gender, talents, conception of good)

Two Principles of Justice (lexical order):
1. Equal Basic Liberties Principle: each person has equal basic liberties compatible with same liberties for all
2. Difference Principle: social/economic inequalities must be:
   (a) attached to offices/positions open to all under fair equality of opportunity
   (b) arranged to benefit the least-advantaged members of society (maximin strategy)

Priority rule: Principle 1 > Principle 2(a) > Principle 2(b)
Critique (Nozick): Difference principle violates property rights; entitlement theory alternative
```

## Applied to Classic Dilemmas

### Trolley Problem (runaway trolley, 5 vs 1)

| Framework | Verdict | Reasoning |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Act Utilitarianism | Pull lever (5 > 1) | maximize aggregate welfare |
| Kantian Deontology | DO NOT pull (contested) | using 1 person as means; or: duty to minimize harm (Ross: conflicting duties) |
| Virtue Ethics | Contextual; what would a person of practical wisdom do? | most virtue ethicists: pull lever; focus on agent's motive |
| Care Ethics | Who has relationships? Who is more vulnerable? | resists abstract calculation; focuses on particular relationships |
| Contractualism | Pull lever is permissible; nobody could reasonably reject the principle | Scanlon: but Footbridge variant (push fat man) is impermissible |

**Trolley vs Footbridge distinction (Thomson 1985):** physically pushing someone activates different intuitions than redirecting harm via lever; documented across cultures in empirical moral psychology.

### Heinz Dilemma (steal drug to save wife)

| Framework | Verdict |
|-----------|---------|
| Utilitarianism | Steal: wife's life > drug company's profit |
| Deontology (Kant) | Do not steal (cannot universalize theft); but also duty to preserve life → unresolvable |
| Virtue Ethics | A compassionate person steals; justice requires facing consequences after |
| Care Ethics | Steal: relationship to wife grounds the obligation |
| Contractualism | Drug company cannot reasonably reject a principle permitting theft under life-threatening necessity |

Kohlberg used this dilemma to measure moral development stages: pre-conventional → conventional → post-conventional.

### Corporate Whistleblowing

| Framework | Verdict |
|-----------|---------|
| Utilitarianism | Blow whistle if public harm > personal/company harm; requires estimating probabilities |
| Deontology | Duty of loyalty (contractual) vs duty to prevent harm; prima facie duty to prevent serious harm generally overrides |
| Virtue Ethics | An honest, courageous person speaks truth; integrity demands it |
| Care Ethics | To whom do you owe care? Colleagues at risk vs the public; particular relationships complicate the calculus |
| Contractualism | Nobody could reasonably reject a principle requiring disclosure of serious public harm |

**Source:** Rachels & Rachels "The Elements of Moral Philosophy" 9th ed. + Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy + Rawls "A Theory of Justice" revised ed. (1999)
