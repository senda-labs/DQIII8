# Mechanics Fundamentals

## Definition
Mechanics is the branch of physics that studies the motion of objects and the forces that cause or resist that motion. Classical mechanics (Newtonian) describes macroscopic objects at everyday speeds. Relativistic and quantum mechanics extend this to extreme regimes.

## Core Concepts

- **Newton's Three Laws:**
  1. Inertia: An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted on by a net force.
  2. F = ma: Net force equals mass times acceleration.
  3. Action-Reaction: For every force, there is an equal and opposite reaction force.
- **Kinematics:** Describes motion without reference to forces. Key equations (constant acceleration): v = v0 + at; x = x0 + v0*t + (1/2)at^2; v^2 = v0^2 + 2a*Δx.
- **Work and Energy:** Work = F * d * cos(θ). Kinetic energy KE = (1/2)mv^2. Potential energy PE = mgh (gravitational). Conservation of energy: KE + PE = constant (no friction).
- **Momentum:** p = mv. Conservation of momentum: total momentum is constant in closed systems. Impulse = F*Δt = Δp.
- **Rotational Mechanics:** Angular velocity ω, torque τ = r × F, moment of inertia I, angular momentum L = Iω. Newton's 2nd law for rotation: τ = Iα.
- **Friction:** Static friction fs <= μs * N; kinetic friction fk = μk * N. Static friction can take any value up to its maximum.
- **Oscillation and Waves:** Simple harmonic motion: F = -kx (Hooke's law). Period T = 2π√(m/k). Wave speed v = fλ.

## Key Equations
- Gravitational force: F = G * m1 * m2 / r^2
- Centripetal acceleration: a_c = v^2 / r
- Power: P = F * v = W / t

## Practical Applications
- **Engineering:** Structural analysis of bridges and buildings, machine design.
- **Aerospace:** Orbital mechanics, rocket propulsion (conservation of momentum).
- **Robotics:** Kinematics of robot arms, dynamics of motion control.
- **Sports science:** Projectile motion analysis, impact forces.
- **Gaming:** Physics engines simulate rigid body dynamics.
