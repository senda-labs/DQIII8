# Organic Chemistry Fundamentals

## Definition
Organic chemistry is the study of carbon-containing compounds, their structure, properties, reactions, and synthesis. Carbon's ability to form four covalent bonds and create long chains, rings, and complex 3D structures makes it the basis of all known life and an enormous range of synthetic materials.

## Core Concepts

- **Functional Groups:** Specific groups of atoms that determine chemical behavior. Key groups: hydroxyl (-OH, alcohols), carbonyl (C=O, aldehydes/ketones), carboxyl (-COOH, acids), amino (-NH2, amines), ester (-COO-), ether (-O-).
- **Hydrocarbons:** Compounds of only C and H. Alkanes (single bonds, saturated), alkenes (double bonds), alkynes (triple bonds), aromatics (benzene rings).
- **IUPAC Nomenclature:** Systematic naming of organic compounds. Identifies longest carbon chain, numbering to give substituents lowest numbers, then functional group suffixes.
- **Reaction Types:**
  - Substitution (SN1, SN2): Replace one group with another.
  - Addition: Add atoms/groups across a double or triple bond.
  - Elimination (E1, E2): Remove atoms to form a double bond.
  - Oxidation/Reduction: Change in oxidation state.
  - Condensation: Combine two molecules, releasing water.
- **Stereochemistry:** 3D arrangement of atoms. Chirality (mirror-image isomers, enantiomers), R/S configuration (Cahn-Ingold-Prelog rules). Critical in pharmaceuticals — enantiomers can have very different biological activity.
- **Reaction Mechanisms:** Step-by-step description of how bonds break and form. Uses curved arrows to show electron movement. Involves nucleophiles (electron donors) and electrophiles (electron acceptors).
- **Spectroscopy:** NMR, IR, mass spectrometry used to determine molecular structure.

## Key Reaction Families
- Esterification: acid + alcohol → ester + water (Fischer)
- Aldol condensation: C-C bond formation between carbonyls
- Diels-Alder: [4+2] cycloaddition, forms 6-membered rings

## Practical Applications
- **Pharmaceuticals:** Drug design, synthesis of active compounds.
- **Polymers:** Plastics (polyethylene, PVC), synthetic fibers (nylon, polyester).
- **Agriculture:** Herbicides, pesticides, fertilizer chemistry.
- **Food science:** Flavor compounds, preservatives, Maillard reaction.
- **Biochemistry:** Proteins (amino acids), DNA (nucleotides), lipids, carbohydrates.
