# Narrative Structure — Beats & Reference

## Save the Cat — 15 Beats (110-page screenplay)

| Beat | Page | Function | Failure Mode |
|------|------|----------|-------------|
| 1. Opening Image | 1 | Snapshot of "before world"; tone + theme | generic establishing shot |
| 2. Theme Stated | 5 | Someone (not hero) states the lesson; hero doesn't understand yet | explicit moralizing |
| 3. Set-Up | 1–10 | Hero, flaws, ordinary world, what needs to change, stakes | too much backstory |
| 4. Catalyst | 12 | Inciting incident; life changes irrevocably; hero receives the call | too late (past p.15) |
| 5. Debate | 12–25 | Should I go? Fear, preparation, false starts; internal conflict | skipping doubt entirely |
| 6. Break Into Two | 25 | Hero's active choice to enter new world; end Act 1 | passive hero dragged in |
| 7. B Story | 30 | Subplot begins (usually romance or mentor); carries theme | no thematic purpose |
| 8. Fun and Games | 30–55 | The premise's "promise"; what the trailer sells; upward path | no escalating fun |
| 9. Midpoint | 55 | False victory OR false defeat; stakes raised; hero "thinks" they've won/lost | nothing actually changes |
| 10. Bad Guys Close In | 55–75 | External pressure grows; internal team fractures | purely external conflict only |
| 11. All Is Lost | 75 | Lowest point; "whiff of death" (literal or symbolic) | too mild; hero recovers too fast |
| 12. Dark Night of the Soul | 75–85 | Emotional rock-bottom; old way has failed; moment of despair | skipped or 2 pages |
| 13. Break Into Three | 85 | A-ha synthesis of A+B story; hero finds new solution/perspective | epiphany unexplained |
| 14. Finale | 85–110 | New plan; storm the castle; high stakes action; catharsis; world changed | montage instead of earned |
| 15. Final Image | 110 | Mirror of Opening Image; shows transformation; proof of theme | identical to opening |

## Hero's Journey (Campbell/Vogler — 12 Stages)

| Stage | Act | Core Action |
|-------|-----|------------|
| Ordinary World | 1 | Hero's normal life established |
| Call to Adventure | 1 | Problem/challenge presented |
| Refusal of the Call | 1 | Fear; reasons to stay |
| Meeting the Mentor | 1 | Guide provides tools/wisdom |
| Crossing the Threshold | 1→2 | Commits to adventure; no return |
| Tests, Allies, Enemies | 2 | Rules of new world learned |
| Approach to the Inmost Cave | 2 | Prep for major ordeal |
| The Ordeal | 2 | Crisis; hero faces death/fear |
| Reward (Seizing the Sword) | 2 | Survives; gains treasure/insight |
| The Road Back | 2→3 | Return journey; stakes raised |
| Resurrection | 3 | Final test; fully transformed |
| Return with the Elixir | 3 | Brings back gift for ordinary world |

## Show Don't Tell — 10 Patterns

| Tell | Show |
|------|------|
| She was angry | She slammed the folder onto the desk, scattering papers across the floor |
| He was nervous | He checked his phone three times in sixty seconds |
| They were in love | She laughed at his terrible jokes; he memorized how she took her coffee |
| The room was filthy | Three empty pizza boxes competed for space with a mountain of unread mail |
| He was powerful | When he walked in, the conversation stopped |
| She was exhausted | She fell asleep mid-sentence, pen still in hand |
| It was cold | He saw his breath and wished he'd worn the other coat |
| They had a bad marriage | They sat through dinner without looking up from their phones |
| The city was dangerous | No one made eye contact. Everyone walked fast |
| He was lying | He looked at the clock twice before answering |

## Character Arc — Three Types

| Arc Type | Beginning | End | Example |
|----------|-----------|-----|---------|
| Positive (Transformation) | flawed, incomplete | overcomes flaw | Walter White (early), Scrooge |
| Negative (Corruption) | moral, good | corrupts/falls | Breaking Bad's Walter White |
| Flat (Test) | already complete | tested, holds values | James Bond, Sherlock Holmes |

**Wound → Lie → Want vs Need:**
- Wound: past trauma that shapes worldview
- Lie: false belief the character holds because of the wound
- Want: conscious goal driving action
- Need: unconscious truth the character must learn; theme of the story

Compelling antagonist rule: the antagonist must believe they are right. Mirror-function: antagonist embodies what the protagonist could become if they don't change.

## Scene-Level Pacing

```
Scene must accomplish minimum 2 of:
1. Advance plot
2. Reveal character
3. Establish/shift relationship
4. Introduce/escalate conflict
5. Establish tone/theme

Scene turns on conflict: enter late, leave early
Goal → Conflict → Outcome (rarely what was wanted)
```
