# Microeconomics Fundamentals

## Definition
Microeconomics studies how individuals, households, and firms make decisions about the allocation of scarce resources. It analyzes supply and demand, market structures, pricing, and the behavior of economic agents at a granular level.

## Core Concepts

- **Supply and Demand:** The law of demand states quantity demanded falls as price rises (ceteris paribus). The law of supply states quantity supplied rises as price rises. Equilibrium where quantity supplied equals quantity demanded.
- **Elasticity:** Measures responsiveness. Price elasticity of demand (PED) = % change in quantity / % change in price. Elastic (|PED| > 1): revenue falls when price rises. Inelastic (|PED| < 1): revenue rises.
- **Consumer Theory:** Utility maximization subject to a budget constraint. Indifference curves represent equal utility combinations. Budget line represents affordability. Optimal consumption at tangency.
- **Producer Theory:** Firms maximize profit = Revenue - Cost. Short-run: at least one fixed input. Long-run: all inputs variable. Economies of scale: AC falls as output rises.
- **Market Structures:**
  - Perfect competition: Many sellers, identical products, price takers, zero long-run profit.
  - Monopoly: Single seller, price maker, produces where MR=MC.
  - Oligopoly: Few large firms, strategic interaction, game theory applies.
  - Monopolistic competition: Many firms, differentiated products, some pricing power.
- **Game Theory:** Strategic decision-making among rational agents. Prisoner's Dilemma, Nash Equilibrium (no player benefits from unilaterally changing strategy).
- **Market Failures:** Externalities (pollution, network effects), public goods (non-rival, non-excludable), information asymmetry, monopoly power require policy intervention.

## Key Curves
- Demand: downward sloping (price vs. quantity)
- Supply: upward sloping
- Marginal cost (MC): U-shaped, intersects AVC and ATC at their minima
- Profit maximization: produce where MR = MC

## Practical Applications
- **Pricing strategy:** Firms use price discrimination, bundling, and elasticity analysis.
- **Policy:** Taxes, subsidies, price controls, antitrust regulation.
- **Platform economics:** Two-sided markets (Uber, Airbnb) — pricing each side optimally.
- **Auction design:** Optimal auction theory for spectrum allocation, online ad auctions.
- **Labor markets:** Wage determination, monopsony in employer power.
