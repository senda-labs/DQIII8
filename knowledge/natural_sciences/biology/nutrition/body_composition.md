# Body Composition

## Body Fat % Classifications (ACE / American Council on Exercise)
| Category | Male | Female |
|----------|------|--------|
| Essential fat | 2–5% | 10–13% |
| Athletes | 6–13% | 14–20% |
| Fitness | 14–17% | 21–24% |
| Average | 18–24% | 25–31% |
| Obese | >25% | >32% |

## Key Equations
```
BMI = weight(kg) / height(m)²
```
- Underweight: <18.5 | Normal: 18.5–24.9 | Overweight: 25–29.9 | Obese: ≥30
- Limitation: does not distinguish fat mass from muscle mass

```
LBM = weight × (1 - BF%)
```
- Lean Body Mass: everything except fat (muscle, bone, organs, water)

```
FFMI = LBM(kg) / height(m)²
```
- Fat-Free Mass Index: muscle mass relative to height
- Natural male upper limit: ~25 FFMI (without PEDs)
- Bodybuilding athletes: 24–26+

## Waist-to-Hip Ratio (WHO cardiovascular risk threshold)
```
WHR = waist circumference / hip circumference
```
- Low risk M: <0.90 | High risk M: ≥0.90
- Low risk F: <0.85 | High risk F: ≥0.85

## Caloric Equivalence
```
1 kg body fat ≈ 7,700 kcal
1 lb body fat ≈ 3,500 kcal
```
- Deficit of 500 kcal/day → ~0.5 kg/week fat loss (theoretical)
- Real-world: slower due to metabolic adaptation, water retention

## Measurement Methods (accuracy)
| Method | Error | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| DEXA scan | ±1–2% | Gold standard, expensive |
| Hydrostatic weighing | ±1.5–2% | Accurate, inconvenient |
| Skinfold calipers | ±3–5% | Operator-dependent |
| BIA (bioelectrical) | ±3–8% | Affected by hydration |
| Navy/BMI formula | ±3–5% | No equipment needed |

## Metabolic Adaptation
During prolonged caloric deficit, TDEE decreases beyond predicted:
- Adaptive thermogenesis: ~100–300 kcal/day reduction
- Reverse dieting needed to restore metabolic rate after extended cuts
