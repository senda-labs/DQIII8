## Toulmin Model (6 Components)

```
CLAIM: What you're arguing (the conclusion)
  ↑
DATA: Evidence/facts supporting the claim
  ↑
WARRANT: The implicit/explicit rule connecting data to claim
  ↑
BACKING: Support for the warrant itself (if challenged)
  ↑
QUALIFIER: Degree of certainty (usually, probably, in most cases)
  ↑
REBUTTAL: Conditions under which claim does NOT hold

Example:
  Claim: Aspirin reduces heart attack risk
  Data: Clinical trial RR=0.76 (95%CI 0.66-0.87)
  Warrant: Statistically significant RCT evidence is reliable
  Backing: Meta-analysis of 18 trials (Antithrombotic Trialists 2002)
  Qualifier: In high-risk patients
  Rebuttal: Unless GI bleeding risk outweighs benefit
```

## 15 Rhetorical Figures

| Figure | Definition | Example |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Anáfora | Repetition at start of successive clauses | "I have a dream... I have a dream..." |
| Metáfora | Implicit comparison; is = | "Life is a journey" |
| Símil | Explicit comparison; like/as | "He fought like a lion" |
| Hipérbole | Extreme exaggeration | "I've told you a million times" |
| Ironía | Meaning opposite to literal | "What lovely weather" (during storm) |
| Antítesis | Contrasting ideas in parallel | "Ask not what your country can do for you..." |
| Quiasmo | Inverted parallel structure (ABBA) | "You can take a horse to water, but you can't make it drink" |
| Paralelismo | Repeated grammatical structure | "Veni, vidi, vici" |
| Asíndeton | Omit conjunctions; staccato effect | "I came, I saw, I conquered" |
| Polisíndeton | Repeat conjunctions; slowing effect | "And the thunder... and the lightning... and the rain..." |
| Metonimia | Replace by associated concept | "The White House announced..." (= President) |
| Sinécdoque | Part for whole (or reverse) | "All hands on deck" (hands = sailors) |
| Personificación | Human qualities to non-human | "The economy is breathing again" |
| Elipsis | Omit words grammatically recoverable | "I'll have the steak [and] you [can have] the fish" |
| Eufemismo | Soften harsh truth | "Passed away" (= died); "downsizing" (= firing) |
