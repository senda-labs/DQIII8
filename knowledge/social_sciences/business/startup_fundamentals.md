# Startup Fundamentals

## Definition
A startup is a newly established business built to grow rapidly by solving a significant problem with a scalable, often technology-enabled solution. Unlike small businesses, startups are designed for exponential growth and typically seek external funding to accelerate.

## Core Concepts

- **Problem-Solution Fit:** Validate that a real, painful problem exists before building. Techniques: customer interviews (mom test), surveys, landing page tests. Goal: understand the problem deeply, not pitch a solution.
- **Product-Market Fit (PMF):** The product satisfies a strong market demand. Indicators: retention curves flatten (users keep returning), NPS > 40, rapid organic growth, users are "very disappointed" if product disappeared (Sean Ellis test). Without PMF, scaling fails.
- **MVP (Minimum Viable Product):** Smallest product that tests a key hypothesis and delivers value. Not a prototype — real users, real feedback. Validate before over-engineering.
- **Build-Measure-Learn Loop (Lean Startup):** Cycle of building a small feature, measuring user behavior, learning from data, and iterating. Reduces waste from building unwanted features.
- **Funding Stages:**
  - Pre-seed/Seed: Idea and early traction. Angels, accelerators (YC, Techstars). $100K–$3M.
  - Series A: Proven PMF, scaling. Institutional VCs. $3M–$15M.
  - Series B+: Scale go-to-market, expand. $15M+.
  - Term sheet, cap table, dilution, liquidation preferences.
- **Unit Economics:** LTV (Lifetime Value) / CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) > 3:1 is healthy. Payback period < 18 months. Contribution margin > 0 for each new customer.
- **Growth:** Acquisition (paid, viral, organic, sales), activation, retention, referral, revenue (AARRR pirate metrics). Identify the one metric that matters (OMTM).
- **Team and Culture:** "Team is everything." Co-founder fit, complementary skills (technical + commercial). Culture defined early becomes hard to change later.

## Key Frameworks
- Jobs-to-be-Done: What job does the customer hire your product to do?
- Value Proposition Canvas: Customer jobs, pains, gains vs. product features, pain relievers, gain creators.
- OKRs (Objectives and Key Results): Set ambitious goals with measurable outcomes.

## Practical Applications
- **Ideation:** Use design thinking, pain point interviews, market gap analysis.
- **Fundraising:** Craft a compelling narrative + data-driven pitch deck (problem, solution, market, traction, team, ask).
- **Hiring:** Early hires disproportionately shape culture — hire for values and potential.
- **Pivot decisions:** When to persist vs. change direction based on evidence.
