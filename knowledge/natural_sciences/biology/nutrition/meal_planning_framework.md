# Meal Planning Framework

## Meal Frequency
- Range: 3–6 meals/day
- Total daily protein is more important than meal timing
- Minimum 3 meals to distribute protein for muscle protein synthesis (MPS)

## Protein Per Meal
```
MPS ceiling per meal: ~40g protein
```
- Beyond 40g per meal, MPS does not increase further
- Distribute protein across 4–5 meals for maximum MPS
- Minimum effective dose per meal: ~20g (0.24 g/kg)

## Nutrient Timing

### Pre-Workout
- Timing: 1–4 hours before training
- Composition: carbohydrates (primary) + moderate protein
- Example: oats + whey, rice + chicken
- If <60 min before: fast-digesting carbs only (banana, sports drink)

### Post-Workout
- Timing: within 2 hours (window is wider than previously believed)
- Protein: 20–40g high-quality protein
- Carbohydrates: 0.8–1.2 g/kg to restore glycogen
- Example: rice + lean protein + vegetables

## Hydration Targets
```
Minimum: 30–35 ml/kg/day
Active individuals: 35–45 ml/kg/day
```
- Add 500 ml per hour of moderate exercise
- Urine color guide: pale yellow = adequate, dark = dehydrated
- Electrolytes needed for sessions >60 min (sodium, potassium)

## Fiber Targets (USDA Dietary Guidelines 2020-2025)
```
Female: 25 g/day
Male:   38 g/day
```
- Soluble fiber: oats, legumes, fruit (gut microbiome, cholesterol)
- Insoluble fiber: vegetables, whole grains (digestive transit)
- Increase gradually to avoid GI distress

## Weekly Meal Structure Template
```
Protein sources: rotate minimum 3 different sources/week
  (chicken, beef, fish, eggs, legumes, dairy)
Carb sources: 2–3 types (rice, oats, potato, quinoa)
Vegetables: minimum 5 servings/day, variety of colors
```

## Batch Cooking Strategy
1. Bulk cook 2–3 protein sources (2–3 days supply)
2. Cook carb base in large batches (refrigerates 4–5 days)
3. Prep vegetables: wash, chop, store raw
4. Assemble meals day-of in <5 minutes
5. Rotate proteins weekly to prevent taste fatigue

## Budget Optimization
- Highest protein/cost: eggs (~$0.10/10g protein), canned tuna, legumes
- Frozen vegetables = same nutritional value as fresh, lower cost
- Buy protein in bulk (chicken breast, ground beef), freeze portions
