# Cell Biology Fundamentals

## Definition
Cell biology studies the structure, function, and behavior of cells — the fundamental unit of life. All living organisms are composed of one or more cells, and all cellular activity stems from the coordinated action of molecular machinery within the cell.

## Core Concepts

- **Cell Types:** Prokaryotic cells (bacteria, archaea) lack a membrane-bound nucleus. Eukaryotic cells (plants, animals, fungi, protists) have a nucleus and membrane-bound organelles.
- **Cell Membrane:** Phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins. Controls what enters and exits the cell. Fluid mosaic model. Transport: passive (diffusion, osmosis, facilitated), active (ATP-driven pumps).
- **Nucleus:** Contains DNA organized into chromosomes. Site of gene transcription (DNA → mRNA). Nuclear membrane separates it from cytoplasm.
- **Organelles:**
  - Mitochondria: "Powerhouse" — produces ATP via cellular respiration (glycolysis, Krebs cycle, oxidative phosphorylation).
  - Ribosomes: Synthesize proteins by translating mRNA (translation).
  - Endoplasmic reticulum (rough ER, smooth ER): Protein and lipid synthesis, transport.
  - Golgi apparatus: Modifies, sorts, and packages proteins for secretion.
  - Lysosomes: Contain enzymes that digest waste molecules.
  - Chloroplasts (plant): Photosynthesis — convert CO2 + H2O + light → glucose + O2.
- **Cell Cycle:** Interphase (G1, S, G2) where cell grows and DNA replicates; Mitosis (M phase) where cell divides. Regulated by cyclins and CDKs. Checkpoints prevent errors.
- **Cell Signaling:** Cells communicate via chemical signals. Receptor → second messenger → kinase cascade → gene expression or metabolic change.
- **DNA Replication and Gene Expression:** DNA → RNA (transcription) → Protein (translation). Central dogma of molecular biology.

## Key Processes
- ATP synthesis: ADP + Pi → ATP (requires energy input)
- Apoptosis: Programmed cell death, essential for development and homeostasis
- Cell division: Mitosis (somatic cells), meiosis (gametes, produces 4 haploid cells)

## Practical Applications
- **Medicine:** Cancer biology (uncontrolled cell division), drug targets, stem cell therapy.
- **Biotechnology:** Recombinant protein production, gene editing (CRISPR-Cas9).
- **Diagnostics:** Flow cytometry, cell culture, microscopy techniques.
- **Agriculture:** Genetically modified crops, tissue culture propagation.
- **Pharmacology:** Understanding drug mechanisms at the cellular level.
