# Business Plan Fundamentals

## Definition
A business plan is a formal document outlining the goals of a business, the strategy for achieving them, the market opportunity, competitive landscape, operations, and financial projections. It serves as a roadmap for the company and a communication tool for investors, partners, and employees.

## Core Concepts

- **Executive Summary:** One-to-two page overview of the entire plan. Covers the problem, solution, target market, business model, traction, team, and funding ask. Written last, read first.
- **Problem and Solution:** Clearly articulate the pain point (customer problem), its scale, and why existing solutions fall short. Present your solution and its unique value proposition.
- **Market Opportunity:** TAM/SAM/SOM analysis. Evidence of market size through bottom-up or top-down estimations. Market trends supporting growth.
- **Business Model:** How the company makes money. Revenue streams (subscription, transaction, licensing, advertising, marketplace). Unit economics: LTV, CAC, gross margin, payback period.
- **Competitive Analysis:** Map competitors on key dimensions. Explain sustainable differentiation — why you win and why it is difficult to copy (moat, network effects, switching costs, brand, IP).
- **Go-to-Market Strategy:** Customer acquisition channels, sales process, partnerships, marketing strategy. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP), customer segments, sales motion (PLG, SLG, channel).
- **Operations Plan:** Key processes, technology stack, supply chain, team structure, milestones.
- **Financial Projections:** 3-5 year income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement. Key assumptions clearly stated. Break-even analysis, burn rate, runway.
- **Team:** Founder/management backgrounds, relevant experience, advisors. Investors bet on the team as much as the idea.

## Common Structures
- Lean Canvas (1-page version): Problem, solution, key metrics, unique value prop, unfair advantage, channels, customer segments, cost structure, revenue streams.
- Full business plan: 15-30 pages with appendices.

## Practical Applications
- **Fundraising:** Basis for investor due diligence. VCs and angels use it to evaluate deals.
- **Bank loans / SBA:** Lenders require financial projections and market analysis.
- **Internal alignment:** Communicates strategy to employees and partners.
- **Grant applications:** Government and foundation grants require detailed plans.
