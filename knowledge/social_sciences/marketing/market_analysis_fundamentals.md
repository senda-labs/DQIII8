# Market Analysis Fundamentals

## Definition
Market analysis is the systematic process of gathering, interpreting, and applying information about a market — its size, segments, competition, trends, and customer needs — to support strategic and business decisions.

## Core Concepts

- **Market Sizing:** Estimating the potential scale of a market. TAM (Total Addressable Market): total demand if 100% market share. SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market): portion you can reach. SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): realistic share.
- **Market Segmentation:** Dividing a heterogeneous market into homogeneous groups. Bases: demographic (age, income, gender), psychographic (values, lifestyle), behavioral (usage, loyalty), geographic.
- **Competitive Analysis:** Understanding strengths and weaknesses of competitors. Porter's Five Forces: competitive rivalry, threat of new entrants, threat of substitutes, buyer power, supplier power. SWOT analysis: internal (strengths, weaknesses) and external (opportunities, threats).
- **Customer Research:** Primary (surveys, interviews, focus groups, ethnography) and secondary (industry reports, government data, competitor analysis). Jobs-to-be-Done theory: understand what customers are trying to achieve.
- **Trend Analysis:** Identify long-term market shifts. PEST/PESTLE framework: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental factors. S-curve adoption models for new technologies.
- **Pricing Analysis:** Price benchmarking, willingness-to-pay research, conjoint analysis, price elasticity estimation.
- **Market Research Methods:** Quantitative (surveys, scanner data, experimental design) and qualitative (focus groups, in-depth interviews, ethnography).

## Frameworks
- TAM/SAM/SOM for opportunity sizing
- Porter's Five Forces for competitive dynamics
- SWOT for strategic position
- PEST for macro environment
- Value chain analysis for competitive advantage sources

## Practical Applications
- **Product launch:** Validate demand, identify target segment, size the opportunity.
- **Pricing strategy:** Competitive pricing, value-based pricing, dynamic pricing.
- **Go-to-market:** Choose channels, messaging, and positioning based on segment research.
- **M&A due diligence:** Assess market attractiveness and competitive position of targets.
- **Investor pitches:** TAM/SAM/SOM slides to demonstrate market opportunity.
