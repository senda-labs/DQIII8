# Nutrition Reference Database

## BMR Formulas — Exact Coefficients

### Mifflin-St Jeor (1990) — Most accurate for general population

```
Men:   BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age(years) + 5
Women: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age(years) − 161
```

### Harris-Benedict Revised (Roza & Shizgal 1984)

```
Men:   BMR = 88.362 + 13.397 × weight(kg) + 4.799 × height(cm) − 5.677 × age
Women: BMR = 447.593 + 9.247 × weight(kg) + 3.098 × height(cm) − 4.330 × age
```

### Katch-McArdle (1996) — Uses lean body mass

```
BMR = 370 + 21.6 × LBM(kg)
LBM = weight × (1 − body_fat_fraction)
```
