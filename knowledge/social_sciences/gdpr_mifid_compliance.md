# GDPR, MiFID II & LCSP Compliance Reference

## GDPR — Key Articles with Actionable Detail

### Article 5 — Data Processing Principles (fines: Art.83(5) ≤€20M or 4%)
1. **Lawfulness, fairness, transparency** — must have legal basis
2. **Purpose limitation** — specified, explicit, legitimate purposes only
3. **Data minimisation** — adequate, relevant, not excessive
4. **Accuracy** — kept up to date; inaccurate data erased/rectified
5. **Storage limitation** — no longer than necessary
6. **Integrity & confidentiality** — appropriate security measures
7. **Accountability** — controller must demonstrate compliance

### Article 6 — Legal Bases for Processing
| Basis | Condition | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Consent (Art.6(1)(a)) | freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous | right to withdraw anytime; no bundled consent |
| Contract (Art.6(1)(b)) | necessary for contract performance | only strictly necessary processing |
| Legal obligation (Art.6(1)(c)) | required by EU/Member State law | must specify the obligation |
| Vital interests (Art.6(1)(d)) | life-or-death situations | last resort; rarely applicable |
| Public task (Art.6(1)(e)) | exercise of official authority | public bodies primarily |
| Legitimate interests (Art.6(1)(f)) | controller/third party interest, not overridden by data subject | three-part test required; NOT available for public authorities |

### Key Obligations
- **Art.25 — Privacy by Design:** appropriate technical measures; data minimisation by default
- **Art.32 — Security:** pseudonymisation, encryption, confidentiality, availability, resilience; regular testing
- **Art.33 — Breach notification:** to supervisory authority ≤72 hours; to data subjects "without undue delay" if high risk
- **Art.35 — DPIA (EIPD):** required for: systematic profiling, large-scale sensitive data, systematic monitoring of public areas
- **Art.37 — DPO required:** public authorities, large-scale systematic monitoring, large-scale sensitive data processing

## MiFID II — Client Classification

| Category | Protection Level | Qualification |
|----------|-----------------|---------------|
| Retail client | Highest (full protection) | default category |
| Professional client | Medium | institutional/large firms; can opt down to retail |
| Eligible counterparty | Lowest | investment firms, credit institutions, insurers |

### Suitability vs Appropriateness
- **Suitability test** (Art.25(2)) — required for investment advice and portfolio management: knowledge+experience, financial situation, investment objectives, sustainability preferences
- **Appropriateness test** (Art.25(3)) — required for execution-only and non-advised services: knowledge and experience only; not financial situation/objectives
- **Execution-only exemption** — no test for non-complex instruments (listed shares, UCITS) if client-initiated

|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| Open above threshold | 35 days (OJ submission) | 15 days |
| Restricted: selection | 30 days | 15 days |
| Restricted: tender | 30 days | 10 days |
| Simplified (below threshold) | 15 working days | — |
| Minor contract | no competitive tender | works <€40k, services/supplies <€15k |

### LCSP Evaluation Criteria
- Economic criteria alone: NEVER >50% (price/cost weighting rule)
- Quality criteria: must be defined objectively
- Abnormally low tenders: must be justified; rejection requires adversarial procedure
