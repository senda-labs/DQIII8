# Allergies and Intolerances

## Allergy vs. Intolerance
| Feature | Allergy (IgE-mediated) | Intolerance (enzymatic/chemical) |
|---------|----------------------|----------------------------------|
| Mechanism | Immune response (IgE) | Enzyme deficiency or chemical sensitivity |
| Onset | Minutes (anaphylaxis possible) | Hours (2–48h) |
| Fatal risk | Yes (anaphylaxis) | No |
| Threshold | Any trace can trigger | Dose-dependent |
| Example | Peanut allergy | Lactose intolerance |

14. Molluscs

## Lactose Intolerance
```
Prevalence: 65–70% of world population
Cause: reduced lactase enzyme production after childhood
```
- Symptoms: bloating, gas, diarrhea (dose-dependent)
- Most people tolerate 12g lactose/meal (one cup of milk)
- Solutions: lactase enzyme supplement, lactose-free products, fermented dairy (yogurt, hard cheese)

## Celiac Disease vs. Non-Celiac Gluten Sensitivity (NCGS)
| Feature | Celiac | NCGS |
|---------|--------|------|
| Mechanism | Autoimmune (anti-tTG antibodies) | Non-autoimmune |
| Prevalence | ~1% population | ~0.5–13% (uncertain) |
| Intestinal damage | Yes (villous atrophy) | No |
| Gluten threshold | Zero (complete elimination) | May tolerate traces |
| Diagnosis | Biopsy + serology | Exclusion of celiac + wheat allergy |

### Gluten Sources
- Contains gluten: wheat, barley, rye, spelt, kamut, triticale
- Gluten-free grains: rice, maize, quinoa, potato, buckwheat, millet, amaranth, oats (if certified GF)

## Cross-Contamination Prevention
- Dedicated cookware, cutting boards, toaster for gluten-free
- Shared fryers: gluten transfer from breaded items
- "May contain traces" labeling: risk for celiac, generally safe for NCGS
- Sulphites: common in wine, dried fruits, processed meats
