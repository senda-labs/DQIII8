# Thermodynamics Fundamentals

## Definition
Thermodynamics studies heat, temperature, energy, and the relationships between them. It describes how energy is transferred between systems and how it can be converted between forms, particularly between heat and mechanical work.

## Core Concepts

- **Zeroth Law:** If two systems are each in thermal equilibrium with a third, they are in equilibrium with each other. This defines temperature.
- **First Law (Energy Conservation):** ΔU = Q - W. Internal energy change equals heat added to the system minus work done by the system. Energy cannot be created or destroyed.
- **Second Law (Entropy):** Heat flows spontaneously from hot to cold, never the reverse. Entropy of an isolated system never decreases. No heat engine can be 100% efficient.
- **Third Law:** Entropy of a perfect crystal approaches zero as temperature approaches absolute zero.
- **Entropy:** Measure of disorder or the number of microstates corresponding to a macrostate. S = k_B * ln(Ω) (Boltzmann). Spontaneous processes increase total entropy.
- **Heat Engines and Cycles:** Convert heat to work. Efficiency η = 1 - T_cold/T_hot (Carnot efficiency — theoretical maximum). Common cycles: Carnot, Otto (gasoline engines), Diesel, Rankine (steam turbines).
- **State Variables:** Temperature T, pressure P, volume V, internal energy U, enthalpy H = U + PV, Gibbs free energy G = H - TS.
- **Ideal Gas Law:** PV = nRT. Relates pressure, volume, temperature, and moles of an ideal gas.

## Key Relations
- Carnot efficiency: η_max = 1 - T_c / T_h
- Clausius inequality: ΔS >= Q/T (equality for reversible processes)
- Maxwell relations: Link partial derivatives of state functions

## Practical Applications
- **Power generation:** Steam turbines, gas turbines, nuclear reactors.
- **Refrigeration and HVAC:** Heat pumps, air conditioners operate as reversed heat engines.
- **Chemical reactions:** Gibbs free energy determines spontaneity (ΔG < 0 = spontaneous).
- **Combustion engines:** Internal combustion engine efficiency governed by Otto cycle.
- **Materials science:** Phase diagrams, solidification, sintering processes.
