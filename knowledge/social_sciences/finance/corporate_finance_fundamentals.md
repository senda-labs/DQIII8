# Corporate Finance Fundamentals

## Definition
Corporate finance deals with how companies fund their activities, make investment decisions, and return value to shareholders. The core objective is maximizing firm value (or shareholder wealth) through optimal decisions about capital structure, investments, and dividends.

## Core Concepts

- **Capital Structure:** Mix of debt and equity financing. Modigliani-Miller theorem: in perfect markets, capital structure is irrelevant. In reality, debt has a tax shield (interest is deductible), but too much debt increases financial distress costs. Optimal structure balances these trade-offs.
- **Valuation:** Determining the intrinsic value of a company or asset.
  - DCF (Discounted Cash Flow): Value = sum of future cash flows discounted at WACC. Sensitive to terminal value assumptions.
  - Comparables (multiples): EV/EBITDA, P/E, P/S ratios vs. peer companies.
  - Precedent transactions: M&A deals in same sector.
- **WACC (Weighted Average Cost of Capital):** Blended cost of equity and debt. WACC = (E/V)*Re + (D/V)*Rd*(1-T). Used as discount rate in DCF. Re from CAPM: Re = Rf + β*(Rm - Rf).
- **Capital Budgeting:** Evaluating long-term investment projects.
  - NPV (Net Present Value): Sum of discounted cash flows minus initial investment. Invest if NPV > 0.
  - IRR (Internal Rate of Return): Discount rate that makes NPV = 0. Accept if IRR > hurdle rate.
  - Payback period: Time to recover initial investment.
- **Working Capital Management:** Managing short-term assets (receivables, inventory) and liabilities (payables). Cash conversion cycle = DIO + DSO - DPO.
- **Dividend Policy:** Return cash to shareholders via dividends or buybacks. Modigliani-Miller dividend irrelevance vs. signaling theory, agency theory.
- **M&A:** Mergers and acquisitions. Synergies (cost reduction, revenue growth) must exceed acquisition premium. Due diligence process evaluates risks.

## Key Metrics
- EV (Enterprise Value) = Market Cap + Debt - Cash
- EBITDA, Free Cash Flow, Return on Invested Capital (ROIC)
- Debt/EBITDA leverage ratio

## Practical Applications
- **Startup funding:** Equity rounds vs. debt financing decisions.
- **IPO:** Valuation, underwriting, float.
- **Private equity:** LBO (leveraged buyout), portfolio management.
- **Strategic decisions:** Build vs. buy, capex allocation.
