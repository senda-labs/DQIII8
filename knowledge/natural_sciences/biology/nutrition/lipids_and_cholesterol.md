# Lipids and Cholesterol

## Clinical Lipid Targets (mg/dL)
| Marker | Optimal | Borderline | High Risk |
|--------|---------|------------|-----------|
| LDL ("bad") | <100 | 130–159 | >160 |
| HDL ("good") | >60 (protective) | 40–59 | <40 |
| Triglycerides | <150 | 150–199 | >200 |
| Total cholesterol | <200 | 200–239 | >240 |
| Total/HDL ratio | <4.0 ideal | 4.0–5.0 | >5.0 |

## Dietary Fat Guidelines

### Saturated Fat (AHA)
```
<10% of total calories
<7% for high cardiovascular risk
1g saturated fat → raises LDL ~0.8 mg/dL
```

### Trans Fat
```
Target: 0g (eliminate completely)
Partially hydrogenated oils: worst for LDL/HDL ratio
WHO: eliminate industrial trans fats globally by 2023
```

### Omega-6 : Omega-3 Ratio
```
Ideal: ≤4:1
Mediterranean diet: ~4:1
Western diet (typical): 15:1–20:1
```
High omega-6:omega-3 ratio promotes systemic inflammation.

## Omega-3 Food Sources (EPA+DHA per 100g)
| Food | EPA+DHA |
|------|---------|
| Salmon (Atlantic, farmed) | 2.2 g |
| Sardines (canned in water) | 1.5 g |
| Mackerel | 2.6 g |
| Herring | 1.7 g |
| Walnuts (ALA only) | 2.6 g ALA |
| Flaxseed (ALA only) | 22.8 g ALA |

Note: ALA (plant) converts to EPA/DHA at ~5–15% efficiency only.

## Supplementation
```
Fish oil for triglyceride reduction: 2–4g EPA+DHA/day
Maintenance/cardiovascular protection: 1–2g EPA+DHA/day
LDL-lowering effect: minimal; triglycerides: –20 to –30%
```
- Prescription omega-3 (Vascepa/icosapentaenoic acid): 4g/day, reduces CV events ~25% (REDUCE-IT trial)
- Krill oil: similar EPA+DHA, better absorption, higher cost

## Constraints
- Dietary cholesterol impact on blood LDL is modest (saturated fat matters more)
- Statins indicated if LDL >190 or 10-year CV risk >7.5% (ACC/AHA guidelines)
