# Financial Statements Analysis

## Three Core Statements
1. **Income Statement**: Revenue → COGS → Gross Profit → OpEx → EBIT → Interest → EBT → Tax → Net Income
2. **Balance Sheet**: Assets = Liabilities + Equity (snapshot)
3. **Cash Flow Statement**: Operating + Investing + Financing = ΔCash

## Key Linkages
- Net Income flows from Income Statement to Retained Earnings (BS)
- Depreciation: non-cash expense on IS, added back in CFO
- CapEx: reduces cash (CFI), increases PP&E (BS)
- Debt issuance: increases cash (CFF), increases liabilities (BS)

## Operating Cash Flow (Indirect Method)
```
CFO = Net Income + Depreciation + ΔWorking Capital
ΔWC = ΔAR + ΔInventory - ΔAP
```
Positive CFO > Net Income = high earnings quality.

## Free Cash Flow
```
FCFF = EBIT×(1-t) + D&A - CapEx - ΔWC
FCFE = FCFF - Interest×(1-t) + Net Borrowing
```
- FCFF: available to all capital providers
- FCFE: available to equity holders only

## Red Flags
- Revenue growing faster than CFO (aggressive accruals)
- Increasing receivables as % of revenue (channel stuffing)
- Capitalizing expenses (inflating assets, understating expenses)
- Off-balance-sheet liabilities (operating leases pre-IFRS 16)
