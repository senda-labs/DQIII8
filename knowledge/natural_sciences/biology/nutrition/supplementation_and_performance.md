# Supplementation and Performance

## Evidence Classification (ISSN)
- Level A: strong evidence, safe and effective
- Level B: limited research, possibly effective
- Level C: little evidence or mixed results
- Level D: no evidence or evidence of harm

## Level A Supplements

### Creatine Monohydrate
```
Dose: 3–5g/day (maintenance; no loading needed)
Loading protocol: 20g/day × 5 days, then 3–5g/day
Effect: +5–10% strength/power, +1–2 kg LBM (water in muscle)
```
- Mechanism: increases phosphocreatine stores for ATP resynthesis
- Safe long-term (up to 5 years studied); no kidney damage in healthy individuals
- Monohydrate = most studied and cheapest form (others offer no advantage)

### Caffeine
```
Dose: 3–6 mg/kg body weight
Timing: 30–60 min pre-exercise
Effect: +3–10% endurance performance, reduces perceived exertion
```
- Half-life: 4–6 hours (avoid if training after 4pm for sleep quality)
- Tolerance develops; cycling (off-weeks) maintains effectiveness
- Anhydrous caffeine = more precise dosing than coffee

### Beta-Alanine
```
Dose: 3.2–6.4g/day (split doses to reduce paresthesia)
Effect: improves performance in 1–4 min sustained efforts
```
- Mechanism: increases muscle carnosine → buffers H⁺ (lactic acid)
- Paresthesia (tingling): harmless, reduced with divided doses or SR formulas
- No acute effect; requires 4–6 weeks loading

## Level B Supplements

### Whey Protein
```
Dose: 20–40g post-workout
Leucine content: >2.5g per dose to maximize MPS
```
- Whey isolate: >90% protein, <1g lactose (suitable for most lactose intolerant)
- Whey concentrate: 70–80% protein, lower cost
- Casein: slow-digesting, useful pre-sleep (40g before bed → MPS overnight)

### Vitamin D
```
Supplement if serum 25(OH)D < 30 ng/mL
Dose: 2000–4000 IU/day (cholecalciferol, D3 preferred over D2)
Target serum level: 40–60 ng/mL
```
- Test annually; over-supplementation risk above 10,000 IU/day sustained

### Omega-3 (Athletes)
```
Dose: 2–4g EPA+DHA/day for anti-inflammatory effect
```
- Reduces DOMS, supports joint health, possible cognitive benefit

### Magnesium
```
Dose: 200–400mg before sleep (glycinate or malate form — best absorption)
Effect: improved sleep quality, muscle recovery, reduces nocturnal cramps
```
- Oxide form: poor absorption (~4%), avoid

### Iron
```
Supplement ONLY if confirmed deficiency: serum ferritin <30 ng/mL
Dose: 18–60mg elemental iron/day (guided by test results)
```
- Excess iron is toxic (hemochromatosis risk)
- Athletes (especially female endurance): test ferritin every 6 months

## Evidence Weak / Insufficient
| Supplement | Verdict |
|------------|---------|
| BCAAs | Redundant if total protein is adequate (≥1.6 g/kg) |
| Glutamine | No performance benefit in healthy athletes |
| Tribulus terrestris | No testosterone effect in humans |
| HMB | Small benefit only in untrained individuals |
| CLA | Minimal fat loss effect; not meaningful |
| Fat burners (most) | Primarily caffeine; rest lacks evidence |

## Safety Principles
- Do not supplement without identified need (excess vitamin A: hepatotoxic; excess iron: oxidative stress)
- Third-party tested brands (NSF, Informed Sport) for competitive athletes
- Supplements do not replace food quality or training consistency
